Begalooru or Bengaluru, also known as Bangalore, is the capital of the southern Indian state of Karnataka. It is known as the "Silicon Valley of India" due to its status as the country's leading IT exporter. The city is also home to a number of historical and cultural attractions.
Weather: Bengaluru has a tropical savanna climate with distinct wet and dry seasons. The best time to visit is from October to February when the weather is pleasant and the city is less crowded.
Tourist Attractions:
Lalbagh Botanical Garden: A 240-acre garden that is home to over 1,800 species of plants and a beautiful glasshouse.
Bangalore Palace: A stunning palace built in 1887 in the Tudor-style architecture with beautiful gardens and antique furniture.
Cubbon Park: A 120-hectare park in the heart of the city that features beautiful greenery and various statues and buildings.
Vidhana Soudha: A massive government building built in 1956 in the neo-Dravidian style.
Tipu Sultan's Summer Palace: A beautiful palace built in 1791 that was used by Tipu Sultan as a summer retreat.
ISKCON Temple: A beautiful temple dedicated to Lord Krishna that features stunning architecture and beautiful gardens.
Ulsoor Lake: A serene lake in the heart of the city that offers boating and other water activities.
Bannerghatta National Park: A national park located just outside the city that is home to a variety of wildlife, including tigers, lions, and elephants.
Wonderla Amusement Park: An amusement park with thrilling rides and water attractions.
Bangalore Fort: A historic fort built in the 16th century that features beautiful architecture and a museum
