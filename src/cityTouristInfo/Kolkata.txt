Kolkata, also known as the City of Joy, is a vibrant city located in the eastern India, in West Bengal. Due to the British influence which one can still see in this city, there are a variety of tourist attractions!
Tourist Attractions:
Victoria Memorial: Victoria Memorial is a famous monument located in the heart of Kolkata. It is known for its beautiful architecture, stunning gardens, and historical significance.
Howrah Bridge: Howrah Bridge is a famous landmark in Kolkata. It is one of the busiest bridges in the world and is known for its unique architecture and stunning views.
Dakshineswar Kali Temple: Dakshineswar Kali Temple is a famous Hindu temple located in Kolkata. It is known for its religious significance, beautiful architecture, and peaceful surroundings.
St. Paul's Cathedral: St. Paul's Cathedral is a famous church located in Kolkata. It is known for its beautiful architecture, historical significance, and peaceful surroundings.
Kalighat Temple: Kalighat Temple is a famous Hindu temple located in Kolkata. It is known for its religious significance, beautiful architecture, and vibrant atmosphere.
Weather: Kolkata has a tropical climate with hot and humid summers and cool winters. The temperature during the summer months, from March to June, can reach up to 40 degrees Celsius. The monsoon season in Kolkata is from June to September, and it brings moderate to heavy rainfall to the region. The best time to visit Kolkata is between October and March, when the weather is cooler and more pleasant, with temperatures ranging from 15 to 25 degrees Celsius.
