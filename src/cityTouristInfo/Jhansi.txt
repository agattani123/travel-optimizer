Jhansi is a historic city in the Indian state of Uttar Pradesh. Balwant Nagar was the old name of Jhansi. It lies in the region of Bundelkhand on the banks of the Pahuj River, in the extreme south of Uttar Pradesh. Jhansi is the administrative headquarters of Jhansi district and Jhansi division.
Tourist Attractions: Jhansi Fort: A historic fort built in the 17th century, known for its impressive architecture and significant role in Indian history.
Rani Mahal: A beautiful palace built in the 18th century, known for its stunning architecture and historic significance.
St. Jude's Shrine: A popular shrine dedicated to St. Jude Thaddeus, known for its spiritual significance and beautiful architecture.
Maharaja Gangadhar Rao ki Chathri: A popular monument dedicated to Maharaja Gangadhar Rao, known for its stunning architecture and historic significance.
Barua Sagar: A popular lake located on the outskirts of Jhansi, known for its beautiful natural setting and popular picnic spot.
Jhansi Museum: A popular museum showcasing the art, culture, and history of Jhansi and its neighboring regions.
Rani Mahal Garden: A beautiful garden located near Rani Mahal, known for its beautiful flowers and plants.
Weather: The city has a tropical savanna climate, with hot summers and mild winters. The best time to visit Jhansi is during the winter months from October to March when the weather is cooler and more pleasant.
