Ujjain is a historical and cultural city located in the central Indian state of Madhya Pradesh. Here are some of the top tourist attractions and information about the weather:
Tourist Attractions: Mahakaleshwar Jyotirlinga Temple - A famous Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, featuring beautiful architecture and a scenic location.
Kaliadeh Palace - A historical palace located on the banks of the Shipra River, featuring beautiful architecture and a serene atmosphere.
Vedh Shala - An ancient observatory dating back to the 18th century, featuring a variety of astronomical instruments and devices.
Chintaman Ganesh Temple - A famous temple dedicated to Lord Ganesha, featuring beautiful architecture and a peaceful atmosphere.
Kal Bhairav Temple - A temple dedicated to Lord Bhairava, known for its unique architecture and religious significance.
Weather: Ujjain has a humid subtropical climate with hot summers and mild winters. The summer season lasts from March to June, with temperatures ranging from 22 to 42 degrees Celsius. The monsoon season lasts from July to September, bringing moderate to heavy rainfall. The winter season lasts from November to February, with temperatures ranging from 6 to 25 degrees Celsius. The best time to visit Ujjain is from October to March when the weather is pleasant and dry.
