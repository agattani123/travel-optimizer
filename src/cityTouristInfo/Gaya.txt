Gaya is a holy city beside the Falgu River, in the northeast Indian state of Bihar.
Tourist Attractions: Mahabodhi Temple: A UNESCO World Heritage Site and popular Buddhist pilgrimage destination, known for its ancient temple and the Bodhi Tree under which the Buddha is said to have attained enlightenment.
Vishnupad Temple: A popular Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu, known for its unique architecture and spiritual significance.
Tibetan Refugee Market: A popular market in Gaya, known for its traditional Tibetan handicrafts and souvenirs.
Barabar Caves: A popular site of ancient rock-cut caves, located on the outskirts of Gaya.
Bodhgaya Archaeological Museum: A popular museum showcasing the art and artifacts of ancient India, located near the Mahabodhi Temple.
Thai Monastery: A popular Buddhist monastery, built in traditional Thai style and located near the Mahabodhi Temple.
Weather: The city has a humid subtropical climate, with hot summers and cool winters. The best time to visit Gaya is during the winter months from October to February when the weather is cooler and more pleasant.
