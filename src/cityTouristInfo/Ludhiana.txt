Ludhiana is a large industrial city in the north Indian state of Punjab. The Punjab Agricultural University is home to the Museum of Rural Life, which displays pottery, musical instruments and traditional Punjabi clothing. Nearby, leafy Nehru Rose Garden features ornamental fountains and more than one thousand different rose varieties. To the north, Maharaja Ranjit Singh War Museum documents Punjabi military history.
Tourist Attractions: Gurudwara Nanaksar Jagraon: A famous Sikh temple known for its stunning architecture and spiritual significance.
Nehru Rose Garden: A popular garden located in the city center, known for its beautiful rose bushes and tranquil surroundings.
Phillaur Fort: A historic fort located on the outskirts of Ludhiana, known for its stunning architecture and historical significance.
Maharaja Ranjit Singh War Museum: A popular museum showcasing the history of the Sikhs and their role in Indian history.
Hardy's World: A popular amusement park located on the outskirts of Ludhiana, known for its thrilling rides and recreational activities.
Rural Olympics: A popular annual event held in Ludhiana showcasing the traditional sports and games of rural Punjab.
Weather:The city has a humid subtropical climate, with hot summers and cool winters. The best time to visit Ludhiana is during the winter months from November to February when the weather is cooler and more pleasant.
