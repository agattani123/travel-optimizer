Jalgaon is a city in Maharashtra, India. The city is located in North Maharashtra, and serves as the administrative headquarters of its namesake district, the Jalgaon district. The Girna river flows from the western part of the city.
Tourist Attractions: Ajanta and Ellora Caves: A UNESCO World Heritage Site and popular tourist destination, located near Jalgaon. The caves are known for their stunning rock-cut temples and sculptures.
Gandhi Research Foundation: A popular museum and research center located in Jalgaon, dedicated to the life and teachings of Mahatma Gandhi.
Mehrun Lake: A popular lake located in the heart of Jalgaon, known for its serene surroundings and recreational activities.
Patnadevi Temple: A famous temple dedicated to Goddess Durga, located on a hilltop near Jalgaon.
Ekvira Devi Temple: A popular temple dedicated to the Hindu goddess Ekvira, located on the banks of the Girna River near Jalgaon.
Shree Manudevi: A popular temple dedicated to the Hindu goddess Manudevi, located in the nearby town of Mehrun.
Weather: The city has a tropical wet and dry climate, with hot summers and mild winters. The best time to visit Jalgaon is during the winter months from November to February when the weather is cooler and more pleasant.
