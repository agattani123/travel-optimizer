Madurai is a city located in the southern Indian state of Tamil Nadu. It is known for its rich cultural heritage, historic temples, and unique architecture. Here are some of the top tourist attractions and information about the weather:
Tourist Attractions: Meenakshi Amman Temple - A historic temple complex dedicated to the goddess Meenakshi, featuring intricate carvings and colorful sculptures.
Thirumalai Nayakkar Mahal - A palace built in the 17th century, known for its impressive architecture and beautiful gardens.
Gandhi Memorial Museum - A museum dedicated to the life and teachings of Mahatma Gandhi, featuring photographs and exhibits.
Vaigai Dam - A large dam built across the Vaigai River, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding hills and valleys.
Koodal Azhagar Temple - A historic temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu, featuring beautiful sculptures and carvings.
Weather: Madurai has a tropical climate with hot summers and mild winters. The summer season lasts from March to June, with temperatures ranging from 28 to 40 degrees Celsius. The monsoon season lasts from July to October, bringing moderate to heavy rainfall. The winter season lasts from November to February, with temperatures ranging from 20 to 30 degrees Celsius. The best time to visit Madurai is from October to March when the weather is pleasant and dry.
