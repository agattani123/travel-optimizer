Bareilly is a city in Bareilly district in the Indian state of Uttar Pradesh. It is among the largest metropolises in Western Uttar Pradesh and is the centre of the Bareilly division as well as the historical region of Rohilkhand.
Tourist Attractions: Trivati Nath Temple - Trivati Nath Temple is an ancient temple located in Bareilly, dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple is known for its beautiful architecture and intricate carvings, and it is a popular place of worship for locals and visitors alike.
Alakhnath Temple - Alakhnath Temple is another ancient temple located in Bareilly, dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple is known for its beautiful architecture and serene atmosphere, and it is a popular place of worship for devotees of Lord Shiva.
Dargah-e-Ala Hazrat - Dargah-e-Ala Hazrat is a famous Sufi shrine located in Bareilly, dedicated to the 19th-century Islamic scholar Imam Ahmed Raza Khan. The shrine is known for its beautiful architecture and peaceful atmosphere, and it is a popular place of worship for Muslims.
Weather: Bareilly experiences a humid subtropical climate with hot summers and cool winters. The summer season starts from March and lasts until June, with temperatures ranging from 28°C to 42°C.
