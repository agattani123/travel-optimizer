 Known as the "Gateway of Northeast India", Shiliguri is located in the Darjeeling and Jalpaiguri districts in the Indian state of West Bengal.
 Tourist Attractions: Mahananda Wildlife Sanctuary: A wildlife sanctuary with a variety of flora and fauna, including tigers, leopards, and deer.
 Kali Mandir: A famous Hindu temple dedicated to the goddess Kali, with intricate carvings and beautiful architecture.
 Salugara Monastery: A Buddhist monastery known for its peaceful surroundings and beautiful gardens.
 ISKCON Temple: A temple dedicated to Lord Krishna, with a beautiful statue of the deity and intricate carvings.
 Coronation Bridge: A historic bridge built over the Teesta River, with stunning views of the surrounding hills.
 Surya Sen Park: A scenic park with a variety of plants and flowers, and a playground for children.
 Science City: A science museum with interactive exhibits and a planetarium.
 Weather: Siliguri is a city located in the northeastern part of India, in the state of West Bengal. The city has a humid subtropical climate, with hot summers and cool winters. The best time to visit Siliguri is during the winter months from October to February when the weather is cooler and more pleasant.
