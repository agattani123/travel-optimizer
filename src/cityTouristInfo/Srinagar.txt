Srinagar is absolutely beautiful city located in the Himalayas, however I would be careful going there around this time
due to increased tensions amongst religious groups and routine terrorism.
Tourist Attractions: Dal Lake - Dal Lake is one of the most famous tourist attractions in Srinagar. Visitors can take a boat ride and explore the lake, which is surrounded by stunning mountains and Mughal-era gardens.
Mughal Gardens - Srinagar is home to several Mughal-era gardens, including Shalimar Bagh, Nishat Bagh, and Chashme Shahi. These gardens are known for their exquisite beauty and are a must-visit for any tourist in Srinagar.
Shankaracharya Temple - The Shankaracharya Temple is a famous Hindu temple located on top of a hill in Srinagar. It offers panoramic views of the city and is a popular pilgrimage site.
Hazratbal Shrine - The Hazratbal Shrine is a revered Muslim shrine in Srinagar, believed to contain a relic of Prophet Muhammad. It is a popular pilgrimage site and is known for its stunning architecture.
Pari Mahal - Pari Mahal is a historic garden located on a hilltop in Srinagar. It was built in the 17th century and offers stunning views of the city and the surrounding mountains.
Wular Lake - Wular Lake is the largest freshwater lake in Asia and is located near Srinagar. Visitors can take a boat ride and explore the lake, which is home to a variety of bird species.
Jama Masjid - Jama Masjid is a historic mosque in Srinagar, built in the 14th century. It is known for its stunning architecture and intricate carvings.
Indira Gandhi Memorial Tulip Garden - The Indira Gandhi Memorial Tulip Garden is a popular tourist attraction in Srinagar, known for its beautiful tulip blooms. It is the largest tulip garden in Asia and is open to visitors from March to May.
Weather: During the winter months (December to February), Srinagar experiences heavy snowfall and temperatures can drop to below freezing, with average daytime temperatures ranging from -2°C to 6°C (28°F to 43°F). The city is covered in a blanket of snow during this time, making it a popular destination for winter sports and tourism.
In the summer months (June to August), Srinagar experiences warm and pleasant weather, with average daytime temperatures ranging from 15°C to 30°C (59°F to 86°F).
