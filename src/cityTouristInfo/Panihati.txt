Panihati is a city and a municipality of North 24 Parganas district in the Indian state of West Bengal.
Tourist Attractions: Dakshineswar Kali Temple: A famous Hindu temple located on the banks of the Hooghly River, dedicated to the goddess Kali.
Belur Math: A monastery founded by Swami Vivekananda, located on the banks of the Hooghly River.
Baranagar Math: A monastery founded by Swami Vivekananda's guru, Sri Ramakrishna.
Howrah Bridge: A famous bridge over the Hooghly River, connecting Kolkata and Howrah.
Kolkata: Panihati is located near Kolkata, the capital city of West Bengal. Kolkata has several popular tourist attractions, including Victoria Memorial, Fort William, and St. Paul's Cathedral.
Sundarbans: A UNESCO World Heritage Site and a popular tourist attraction located near Panihati. Sundarbans is a mangrove forest and home to the Royal Bengal Tiger.
Weather: The city has a tropical climate, with hot and humid summers and mild winters. The best time to visit Panihati is between October and February when the weather is cooler and more pleasant.
