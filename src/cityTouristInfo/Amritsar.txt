Tourist Attractions: Golden Temple - The Golden Temple, also known as Harmandir Sahib, is a holy shrine for the Sikh community and one of the most visited tourist attractions in Amritsar. The temple is known for its stunning architecture, tranquil environment, and the sacred pool that surrounds it.
Jallianwala Bagh - Jallianwala Bagh is a public garden in Amritsar that is known for its historical significance. The garden was the site of the Jallianwala Bagh Massacre, which took place on April 13, 1919, and is remembered as a tragic event in Indian history.
Wagah Border - The Wagah Border is a border crossing between India and Pakistan, located around 30 km from Amritsar. Visitors can witness the famous Beating Retreat Ceremony, which takes place every evening and involves a display of military drills, patriotism, and cultural performances.
Durgiana Temple - Durgiana Temple is a Hindu temple located in the heart of Amritsar, known for its stunning architecture and religious significance. The temple is dedicated to Goddess Durga and is a popular place of worship for locals and visitors alike.
Partition Museum - The Partition Museum is a museum in Amritsar that showcases the history and personal stories of the Partition of India in 1947. The museum has a collection of artifacts, photographs, and personal accounts that provide an insight into the human impact of the event.
