Warangal is the second largest city in Telangana with a population of 704,570 per 2011 Census of India.
Tourist Attractions: Warangal Fort: A historic fort with intricate carvings and ancient architecture.
Thousand Pillar Temple: A Hindu temple with stunning sculptures and carvings.
Bhadrakali Temple: A famous Hindu temple dedicated to the goddess Bhadrakali.
Kakatiya Musical Garden: A beautiful garden with musical fountains and a variety of plants and flowers.
Pakhal Lake: A scenic lake surrounded by lush green forests and hills.
Ramappa Temple: A UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its intricate carvings and unique architecture.
Eturnagaram Wildlife Sanctuary: A wildlife sanctuary with a variety of flora and fauna, including tigers, leopards, and deer.
Weather: The city has a tropical wet and dry climate, with hot summers and mild winters. The best time to visit Warangal is during the winter months from October to February when the weather is cooler and more pleasant.
