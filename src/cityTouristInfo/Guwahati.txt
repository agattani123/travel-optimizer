Guwahati is a vibrant city located in the northeastern state of Assam, India. Here are some of the top tourist attractions and information about the weather:
Tourist Attractions: Kamakhya Temple - A famous Hindu temple dedicated to the goddess Kamakhya, featuring beautiful architecture and a scenic hilltop location.
Umananda Island - A small island located in the middle of the Brahmaputra River, featuring the Umananda Temple and scenic views of the river and the city.
Assam State Museum - A museum showcasing the history, culture, and art of Assam, featuring ancient artifacts, manuscripts, and handicrafts.
Assam State Zoo cum Botanical Garden - A popular zoo and botanical garden featuring a wide variety of animals and plants, including rare and endangered species.
Peacock Island - An island located in the middle of the Brahmaputra River, known for its scenic beauty, peacocks, and the Shiva Temple.
Weather: Guwahati has a humid subtropical climate with hot summers and mild winters. The summer season lasts from March to June, with temperatures ranging from 25 to 38 degrees Celsius. The monsoon season lasts from July to September, bringing heavy rainfall and occasional flooding. The winter season lasts from November to February, with temperatures ranging from 10 to 25 degrees Celsius. The best time to visit Guwahati is from October to April when the weather is pleasant and dry.
