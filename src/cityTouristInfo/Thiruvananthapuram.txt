Thiruvananthapuram (or Trivandrum) is the capital of the southern Indian state of Kerala. It's distinguished by its British colonial architecture and many art galleries. It’s also home to Kuthira Malika (or Puthen Malika) Palace, adorned with carved horses and displaying collections related to the Travancore royal family, whose regional capital was here from the 18th–20th centuries.
Tourist Attractions: Padmanabhaswamy Temple: A famous Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu, known for its stunning architecture and spiritual significance.
Kovalam Beach: A popular beach located on the outskirts of Thiruvananthapuram, known for its beautiful golden sands and serene surroundings.
Napier Museum: A popular museum showcasing the art, culture, and history of Kerala.
Sri Chitra Art Gallery: A popular gallery showcasing the works of famous Indian artists, including Raja Ravi Varma.
Veli Tourist Village: A popular recreational spot located on the outskirts of Thiruvananthapuram, known for its boating facilities and beautiful natural setting.
Shangumugham Beach: A popular beach located near the city center, known for its beautiful views and serene surroundings.
Agasthyakoodam: A popular trekking spot located on the outskirts of Thiruvananthapuram, known for its beautiful views and natural setting.
Weather: The city has a tropical climate, with hot and humid summers and pleasant winters. The best time to visit Thiruvananthapuram is during the winter months from October to February when the weather is cooler and more pleasant.
