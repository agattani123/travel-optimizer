Many temples built from sandstone are dotted around Bindu Sagar Lake in the old city, including the 11th-century Hindu Lingaraja Temple. Outside Rajarani Temple are sculpted figures of the guardians of the 8 cardinal and ordinal directions. Jain antiques, weaponry and indigenous pattachitra paintings fill the Odisha State Museum.
Tourist Attractions:  Lingaraj Temple: A famous Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, known for its stunning architecture and spiritual significance.
Udayagiri and Khandagiri Caves: A popular site of ancient Jain and Buddhist rock-cut temples and caves, located on the outskirts of Bhubaneswar.
Dhauli Hill: A popular hilltop located on the outskirts of Bhubaneswar, known for its ancient rock edicts and the Shanti Stupa, a peace pagoda.
Nandankanan Zoological Park: A popular zoo and botanical garden located on the outskirts of Bhubaneswar, known for its rare species of animals and plants.
Odisha State Museum: A popular museum showcasing the art, culture, and history of Odisha.
Mukteswara Temple: A famous temple known for its intricate carvings and architectural beauty.
Weather: The city has a tropical savanna climate, with hot summers and mild winters. The best time to visit Bhubaneswar is during the winter months from October to February when the weather is cooler and more pleasant.
